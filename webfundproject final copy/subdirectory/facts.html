Facts About the Elephants

1. Type of Elephants:
There are 3 main types of elephants: the Asian elephant, the African bush elephant and the African forest elephant. African bush elephants are the largest of the elephants and have large flappy ears. African forest elephant are the smaller than their bush cousins and have smaller ears with tusks that are pointed down. Asian elephants are the smallest of the elephants and have small ears with tusks that are pointed up [1].

2. Hearing through their feet:
In the year 200, a biologist at Stanford University, Caitlin O'Connell-Rodwell, discovered that elephants can detect the lower frequency vocalizations and foot stomping of elephants resonate, allowing them to communicate through seismic waveforms [2]

3. Wealth of Knowledge:
Elephants have the largest brain of all the land mammals, and they put it to good use. They can recall distant watering holes and even humans that they have encountered years ago. They are also able to transmit certain knowledge such as the path to food and water to their offsprings [3].

4. Math Experts:
According to researchers in Japan, Asian elephants are shown to be able to display basic maths skills, such as choosing panels that displays the most fruits. Furthermore, some research also shows that the average EQ for Asian Elephants is 2.14 while their African counterparts have an EQ of 1.67 [4]

5. Honoring the Dead:
Like humans, elephants are shown to interact with the dead, such as touching, examining and smelling them. Elephants have also made repeated visits and attempted to assist the deceased animal, even calling out for help in some cases [5].
